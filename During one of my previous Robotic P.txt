During one of my previous Robotic Process Automation (RPA) projects, I was responsible for the administration, operational support, and monitoring of more than 50 virtual machines running production automation processes as part of a project for a fraud prevention and security organization.
At that time, one of the tools we used was Remote Desktop Connection Manager (RDCMan), which provided centralized access to multiple remote servers and helped us monitor the environments where production robots were running.

Although today's infrastructure management platforms offer far more advanced capabilities, that experience gave me a strong foundation in enterprise RPA operations and reinforced a principle that remains true today: successful automation depends not only on well-designed bots, but also on reliable infrastructure and effective operational monitoring.
I'm sharing this short demonstration as a reflection of that professional experience, which strengthened my expertise in infrastructure monitoring, operational support, and enterprise automation environments.
